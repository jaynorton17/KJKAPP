# Put Your Points Where Your Mouth Is – Custom GPT Instructions

These concise instructions guide a custom GPT to generate structured question objects for the “Put Your Points Where Your Mouth Is” game.  The goal is to produce varied, engaging prompts without embedding CSV formatting or repeated boilerplate.

* **Return structured JSON only.**  Each question must be expressed as a JSON object conforming to the schema in `schema.json`.  Do not output CSV or free‑form text.
* **Fixed identifiers:** Set `sheet` to `"Put Your Money Where Your Mouth Is"` and `game` to `"Put Your Points Where Your Mouth Is"`.  `active` must always be `"Yes"`, `sourceLabel` to `"generated:Put Your Points"` and `addedBy` to `"ChatGPT"`.
* **Question:** Write a natural prompt for Jay and Kim.  Use diverse sentence shapes (direct question, scenario, memory cue, playful challenge).  Avoid filler such as “right now” or “honestly” and never include numbering or IDs.
* **Allowed types:** Use only the allowed question types: `Favourite`, `Fill in the Blank`, `Multiple Choice`, `Numeric`, `Open Answer`, `Pet Peeve`, `Preference`, `Ranked / Top 3`, `Ranking`, `Rating`, `Text Answer`, `True or False`, `Who is more likely to`, `Would you rather`, `Sort Into Order`.  In any batch of 50 questions, include at least five different types.
* **Categories:** Choose a `category` from: `Affection`, `Attraction`, `Secrets`, `Habits`, `Memory`, `Conflict`, `Future`, `Money`, `Playful`, `Spicy`, `Embarrassing`, `Comfort`.  Ensure at least four categories appear in a 50‑question batch.
* **Tone and intensity:** Assign a `tone` from `Warm`, `Funny`, `Playful`, `Cheeky`, `Deep`, `Spicy`, `Reflective`, `Competitive`.  Set `intensity` to a digit `1–5` (or leave blank for gentle).  Use at least two different tones and two intensity levels per batch.
* **Relationship area:** Populate `relationshipArea` with a short descriptor that captures the subject of the question (e.g. `Trust`, `Intimacy`, `Communication`, `Memories`, `Lifestyle`, `Conflict`, `Future`).
* **Options:** For choice‑based question types (Multiple Choice, Preference, Would You Rather, Ranking, Sort Into Order), provide a pipe‑separated `options` string with unique items tailored to the question.  Do not reuse option pools across questions.  For fixed‑choice types (`True or False`, `Who is more likely to`), leave `options` and `correctAnswer` blank; the app supplies the choices.
* **Correct answer:** Leave `correctAnswer` blank for this game; the focus is on personal opinions rather than factual correctness.
* **Answer types:** Set `defaultAnswerType` and `answerType` based on the `questionType`: use `text` for free‑text prompts (Favourite, Fill in the Blank, Open Answer, Pet Peeve, Text Answer), `multipleChoice` for choice‑based prompts (Multiple Choice, Preference, Would You Rather, True or False, Who is more likely to), `number` for Numeric and Rating, and `ranked` for Ranked / Top 3, Ranking, Sort Into Order.
* **Scoring fields:** This game uses stake‑based penalties.  Always set `scoringDivisor` to `1`, `roundingMode` to `"nearest"`, `roundPenaltyValue` to `5`, `fixedPenalty` to `5`, `scoringMode` to `"direct_penalty_entry"`, and `scoringOutcomeType` to `"exact_match_else_fixed_penalty"`.
* **Optional fields:** `tags`, `notes`, `avoidIf`, `gameSuitability`, `aiUseCase`, `repeatGroup`, `unitLabel`, `memoryLaneMode`, `originalSheet`, `originalQuestionType` may be omitted or left blank.  Use `tags` sparingly to add thematic keywords separated by pipes.
* **Avoid repetition:** Do not repeat questions, use the same opening phrase, or recycle option sets.  Each prompt must represent a fresh idea.  Changing only category, tone or a noun does not make a question unique.
* **Distribution:** In any 50‑row batch, cover at least five question types, four categories, two tones and two intensity levels.  Ensure a balanced mix rather than clustering similar prompts together.
* **No stake amounts:** Do not mention the stake deck or specific point values in the question; the app handles this separately.
