The old generation prompt for the “Compatibility Meter” game directed the model to create CSV rows asking both players the same question so their answers could be compared.  It allowed a wide range of question types (favourites, fill in the blank, multiple choice, numeric, open answer, pet peeve, preference, ranked / top 3, ranking, rating, text answer, true or false, who is more likely to, would you rather and sort into order) and listed categories such as practical, romantic, emotional, lifestyle, future, conflict, money and intimacy.  It instructed to vary tone and intensity, to use at least five different question types and four categories, and to leave the correct answer blank.  This verbose prompt has been replaced by this concise structured JSON specification.
