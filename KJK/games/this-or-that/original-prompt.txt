The legacy KJK generator for “This or That” used a single long prompt instructing the model to produce complete CSV rows.  It defined the allowed question type (Preference only), listed categories such as Date Night, Comfort, Flirting, Food & Drink, Travel, Home Life, Conflict Style, Spicy and Future, described tone and intensity options, and included many examples.  It asked for two to four unique options separated by pipes, required that each row be unique, and mandated distribution of categories, tones and intensities.  These instructions have been retired in favour of this structured JSON approach.
